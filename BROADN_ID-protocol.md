Introducing the BROADN ID System: Enhancing Data Management, Consistency, and Sample Traceability

**Introduction**

The BROADN ID system was created to establish a reliable and consistent method for tracking samples and their derived products. By assigning a unique identifier to each sample, consisting of a specimen code and a derivative code, we ensure that all data remain traceable to the original specimen. This approach allows for quick and easy determination of the sample type and its origin.

## **Importance of Data Consistency**

It is crucial to avoid assigning multiple unrelated IDs to products derived from the same sample or to the sample itself. Inconsistent labeling can lead to confusion, both in the present and future. Our primary goal is to maintain a legible and structured database system that remains reliable and accessible to new researchers joining the project or as the project expands. By ensuring data consistency, we aim to preserve the integrity and usability of past data.

## **Emphasis on Data Quality and Sample Longevity**

Implementing the BROADN ID system and a centralized database is a significant step towards improving data quality and ensuring the longevity of samples. We recognize the importance of these changes and their potential impact on research outcomes and future collaborations.

## **Transition and Support**

We acknowledge that adapting to a new ID system and centralized database may require an adjustment period for many individuals' current workflows. Rest assured that our team will provide the necessary support and guidance throughout this transition to ensure smooth implementation.

## **Changes to Collection Efforts**

The introduction of the BROADN ID system will also bring changes to the way collections are conducted. The Data Management team will play a more active role in sample collections, adding an additional step that primarily involves communication and coordination.

*ID numbers must be requested and allocated before each collection campaign*.

## **Consideration of Current Labeling Practices**

During the development of the BROADN ID system, we carefully considered the current labeling and differentiation practices employed by our team members. These practices played a significant role in the design of location codes and the inclusion of a sample type identifier within the ID structure.

## **Specimen ID**

The specimen ID consists of the first 7 characters of the BROADN ID. This identifier is assigned to the original field sample and serves as a unique reference to the sample's origin. Field samples will only possess a specimen ID.

## **Specimen \+ Derivative ID**

The complete BROADN ID consists of the specimen ID followed by a derivative code. This full identifier is assigned to all products derived from the original field sample, allowing for easy traceability and linkage to the source specimen.

By adhering to the BROADN ID system and utilizing the centralized database, we aim to enhance the quality, consistency, and longevity of our data. This, in turn, will facilitate better collaboration, data sharing, and research outcomes. We appreciate your cooperation and understanding as we implement these important changes to our data management practices.

# **BROADN ID Formatting**

The BROADN ID system consists of two main components: the Specimen ID and the Derivative ID. The Specimen ID is assigned to each field sample collected, while the Derivative ID is used for any product derived from the original field sample.

## **Specimen ID Format** 

The Specimen ID follows the format: 

BCollectionSiteSpecificSiteIDNumberSampleType

·      **"B"** indicates that the sample is "BROADN" collected and documented.   
·      **"Collection Site"** represents the site where the sample was collected (refer to Appendix A).   
·      **"Specific Site"** designates a more precise location within the collection site (refer to *Appendix A*).   
o   The combination of Collection Site and Specific Site forms a 2-character code that determines the **ID number**. *These numbers must be requested and allocated before each collection campaign*.  
·      **"Sample Type"** includes air filters, soil, plant, and liquid (refer to *Appendix B*).

*Example:*  

·      BSE0042P is the 42nd field sample collected from **S**GRC-**E**ast.  It is **P**lant material.    
·      BSE0043F is the 43rd field sample collected from SGRC-East.  It is from a **F**ilter.

## **Derivative Product ID Format**

Any product derived from an original field sample will adhere to the following format:

Specimen ID \+ Derivative Product Code

BCollectionSiteSpecificSiteIDNumberSampleType.DerivativeProductTypeDuplicateNumber

·      The **"Derivative Product Type "** is represented by a one-character code (refer to *Appendix C*).  
·      The **"Duplicate Number"** is a two-digit number used for samples that have the same derivative product type originating from the same field specimen.

*Example:*  

·      BSE0042P.G01 is the first **G**lycerol stock made from BSE0042P.  
·      BSE0042P.D01 is the first **D**NA product made from BSE0042P.

# **Sample Metadata in Data Sampling Spreadsheet**

To ensure comprehensive documentation and traceability of each sample, the following details will be included in the metadata of the data sampling spreadsheet:

1. **BROADN ID:**  The unique identifier assigned to each sample, following the BROADN ID formatting guidelines.  
2. **Original ID:**  Any pre-existing identification number or label associated with the sample before the assignment of the BROADN ID.  
3. **Sample Category:**  The type of sample collected such as air filter, soil, plant, or liquid (refer to Appendix B for sample type codes).  
4. **Purpose:**  A brief description of the sample's intended use or the reason for its collection.  
5. **Sampling Method:**  The technique, procedure, or specific sampler used to collect the sample, such as SASS, Mini SASS, or other relevant methods, including filter type.  
6. **Derivative Product type:**  The product derivative that the sample is stored or preserved as, such as glycerol stock or DNA stock (refer to Appendix C for product derivative codes).  
7. **Date Created:**  The date on which the sample was collected or derived.  
8. **Created By:**  The name of the individual or team responsible for collecting or deriving the sample.  
9. **Storage Location:**  The standardized physical location where the sample is stored, including facility, room, and specific storage conditions (e.g., freezer, cabinet).  *Standardized format to be determined*.  
10. **Originating Location:**  The geographic location or site where the sample was originally collected (refer to Appendix A for collection site codes).  
11. **Project Lead:**  The name of the principal investigator or project leader overseeing the research or study associated with the sample.  
12. **Sequencing information:**  Several columns indicating if sequencing is intended, in-progress, or where sequencing information is stored.  
13. **Additional columns:**  *To be determined.*

Additional metadata fields may be included as needed to capture relevant information specific to each project or study. The metadata should be consistently recorded in the data sampling spreadsheet to facilitate data analysis, interpretation, and future reference.

By incorporating these metadata fields, the data sampling spreadsheet will serve as a comprehensive resource for sample information, enabling efficient tracking, organization, and management of the collected samples throughout the research process.

# **Additional Notes**

## **Transitioning Existing Samples**

The transition of existing samples to the BROADN ID system requires careful attention to maintain data integrity and ensure proper sample tracking. This process will be implemented in phases.  

## **Handling Exceptions and Special Cases**

The BROADN ID system must accommodate exceptional cases while maintaining data integrity and traceability, including unusual sample types, samples not fitting existing categories, mixed sample types, and novel collection methods.
